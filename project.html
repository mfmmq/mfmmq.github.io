CFD-Assisted Study of Diesel Emission Generation
Engines and Combustion Group, University of Oxford, 2017-2018

While experimental results are often more indicative of engine characteristics and performance than numerical simulations, they are also far more costly to undertake and offer less of a view of internal workings. Coupled with simulation however, a better understanding of the root thermodynamic causes can be gained. Analysing how different engine configurations affected performance and emission levels. This involed running test simulations, validating results with test engine data, and applying fundamental thermofluid theory to large datasets.
